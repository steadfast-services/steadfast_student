Section 01
The Two-Pillar Partnership Strategy
Every commission dollar Steadfast earns flows through one of two channels. You need both. They serve different purposes and activate at different times.
01
Aggregator Platform Credentials
These are the master networks — ApplyBoard, Adventus, Shorelight, IDP — that give you instant access to hundreds of university partners through a single portal. You apply once and gain access to many. Commissions flow through the network (10–15% first-year tuition, split with the aggregator). Start here on Day 1. These are your fastest path to being operational and generating commission revenue.
02
Direct University Partnerships
Direct relationships with a school's International Admissions or Global Partnerships office. You are added to their authorized agent list and submit applications directly — cutting out the aggregator's commission split (25–40%). This takes longer to build (3–12 months per school) but yields significantly higher net commission per placed student. Start pursuing these in Month 2–3, while your aggregator pipeline is live.

Strategic Sequence: Apply to all aggregator platforms in Weeks 1–3 so you have an active pipeline immediately. Begin direct university outreach in Month 2 using the email templates in Section 4. By Month 6, aim to have 3–5 direct relationships live to reduce your dependence on the aggregator split.

What You Need Before You Can Apply to Either

Business Credentials

  • Registered LLC or business entity (EIN required)
  • Professional website — even a simple landing page with your services, contact, and about page
  • Professional email address (@steadfaststudentservices.com)
  • Business bank account (separate from personal)
  • LinkedIn company page for Steadfast Student Services

Documentation Aggregators Will Request

  • Government-issued ID (passport or driver's license)
  • Proof of business registration (EIN letter or LLC certificate)
  • Brief company bio / background statement
  • Countries of operation you intend to recruit from
  • Description of your recruitment methods (digital, in-person, referral partners)
  • References — if available (not always required for first-time agents)

Aggregator Platform Partnerships
Apply to these platforms in priority order. Platforms 1–3 are your immediate income engine. Platforms 4–7 expand your university portfolio and geographic reach over time.
ApplyBoard
Largest Agent Network in North America · 1,500+ University Partners
Priority 1 — Apply First
Who to Contact
Recruitment Partner Team via agent signup portal on applyboard.com — search "ApplyBoard become a recruitment partner"
Commission Structure
Varies by school. Typical range: 8–12% of first-year tuition paid to agent. ApplyBoard retains their platform fee separately from the school commission.
Approval Timeline
5–14 business days after completing the application and verification
Step-by-Step: How to Apply
  • 1
    Go to applyboard.com → click "Partners" or "Recruitment Partners" in the footer or main nav → select "Become a Recruitment Partner"
  • 2
    Complete the online agent application: company name, website, EIN or business registration, countries you recruit from, and estimated annual student volume
  • 3
    Upload ID and business registration documents when prompted
  • 4
    Complete ApplyBoard's online agent training modules — mandatory before your account is activated (usually 2–4 hours, free)
  • 5
    Account activated → you can immediately browse all partner schools, submit applications, and track commission status in your dashboard
Pro Tip: When asked about student volume, estimate conservatively (10–20 students/year to start). Overpromising early and underdelivering can flag your account. You can grow the relationship as your volume increases.
Adventus.io

Why Adventus Matters: Adventus shows you the commission rate upfront before applying. Use this to prioritize which schools to actively recruit for based on tuition levels and commission percentage. High-tuition schools = higher absolute commission even at the same percentage.

How Direct University Partnerships Work: Each school maintains an "Authorized Agent List" managed by their International Admissions or Global Partnerships office. You apply via a form on their website (or by emailing their DSO/international director). Follow-Up Protocol: If you receive no response after 7 business days, send one polite follow-up email. If still no response after another 7 days, call the main office and ask to be directed to the International Admissions or Global Partnerships contact. Week-by-Week Partnership Acquisition Timeline
A 12-month sequenced roadmap — what to do, in what order, and why the sequence matters.

Month 1 — Foundation Week by Week

Week 1
Business Setup & Digital Presence
  • Register LLC (use your state's Secretary of State online portal — $50–$200)
  • Apply for EIN via IRS.gov (free, instant online)
  • Set up Google Workspace: admin@steadfaststudentservices.com
  • Create LinkedIn company page for Steadfast Student Services
  • Launch basic website (3 pages: Home, Services, Contact) — Squarespace or Wix if no developer
  • Open business bank account (Chase Business Checking or similar)
Week 2
Apply to Priority 1 Aggregators
  • Day 1: Submit ApplyBoard agent registration — complete all documentation and training modules same week
  • Day 2: Submit Adventus.io agent registration — upload business credentials and complete orientation
  • Day 3: Draft your agency bio and countries-of-recruitment list (you'll reuse this for every application)
  • Set up a tracking spreadsheet: Platform, Date Applied, Status, Contact Name, Follow-Up Date
Week 3
First Wave University Emails — Tier 1 Schools
  • Send Template B to: ASU, University of Arizona, Wichita State, UIW, Fairleigh Dickinson, Pace University
  • Send Template A to: Shorelight (if not fully self-service on their portal)
  • Log every email sent in tracking spreadsheet with date and contact name
  • Set 7-day follow-up reminders in your calendar for every outreach
Week 4
Follow-Up & Priority 2 Aggregators
  • Follow up on any Week 3 university emails with no response
  • Apply to IDP Education agent program
  • Apply to Study Group agent portal
  • Apply to INTO University Partnerships counsellor portal
  • Begin training on ApplyBoard platform (if approved) — learn university search, application submission, commission tracking

Month 2 — Pipeline Activation

Weeks 5–6
Tier 1 University Agreements & First Student Submissions
  • Review and sign any MOU agreements received from Tier 1 schools (read commission payment timing and student refund clauses carefully)
  • Begin submitting your first student applications through ApplyBoard and Adventus — even if just 1–2 students, this establishes your activity record on both platforms
  • Send Template B to remaining Tier 1 schools: University of South Florida, University of Delaware
  • Send Template B to Tier 2 schools: University of New Haven, Lynn University, Pace University, University of Bridgeport
Weeks 7–8
Community College Outreach Begins
  • Send Template C to all 6 community colleges: NOVA, Miami Dade, Houston CC, Dallas College, PCC, ACC
  • Apply to Navitas agent portal and EduCo Pathways portal
  • Apply to Kaplan International Pathways agent program
  • Follow up on all outstanding university applications (any sent 7+ days ago with no reply)

Month 3–4 — Scale & Solidify

Month 3
Remaining Tier 2 & 3 Universities + Columbus State CC
  • Send outreach to: University of Utah, Missouri S&T, George Mason (via INTO), University of Illinois Chicago, University of Alabama (via Shorelight)
  • Follow up on all community college outreach — call any that haven't responded to email
  • Sign and execute all MOU agreements received — file signed copies in your Google Drive
  • Goal by end of Month 3: Active credentials on at least 3 aggregator platforms + 4–6 universities in negotiation or signed
Month 4
NAFSA Conference Registration & Relationship Building
  • Register for the nearest NAFSA Regional Conference (held in Fall/Spring each year — conferences.nafsa.org)
  • Attend regional event to meet international admissions staff in person — this accelerates direct partnerships dramatically
  • Business cards, one-pager on Steadfast, and your agent credential list all ready to share
  • Follow up within 48 hours of meeting any university or college contact at the conference

Month 6 Milestone Goals

By the end of Month 6, you should have: ✦ Active credentials on 5+ aggregator platforms ✦ Signed agent MOUs with at least 8–10 universities and colleges ✦ Applications submitted for at least 5–10 students ✦ At least 1–2 confirmed enrollments generating your first commission payments ✦ A functional CRM tracking every application, school relationship, and commission status

Month 7–12 — Deepening & Diversifying

Q3–Q4
Convert Aggregator Relationships to Direct Agreements
  • For any school where you've placed 1+ students via aggregator, email the school's international office directly to discuss a direct agent MOU — you can now cite your placed student as a reference
  • A direct agreement saves you 25–40% of commission that currently goes to the aggregator platform split
  • Attend NAFSA Annual Conference (May — largest international education conference in the world) — register early, budget ~$1,200–$1,800 total including registration and travel
  • Target 3 new direct university agreements from NAFSA connections
  • Evaluate performance by country: which source markets are generating the most placements? Deepen agent recruitment in those countries first.
