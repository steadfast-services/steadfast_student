What Consular Officers Are Really Looking For
The 3 Systemic Blockers We Resolve
🔗
Weak Home-Country Ties
Under Section 214(b), every applicant is presumed to intend to immigrate. You must prove compelling reasons to return — family, property, job offer, community standing. Most applicants fail to build this case with evidence.
Cited in 60%+ of 214(b) denials
💰
Financial Documentation Failures
Bank statements that look "too new," funds with no clear source, or sponsor letters that don't match I-20 financial requirements — any of these can end an application before the interview even begins.
Flagged in majority of I-20 delays
🗣️
Interview Unpreparedness
Consular officers make decisions in 2–3 minutes. Hesitation, inconsistency, or vague answers on career goals and school choice are red flags. Most applicants have never practiced under realistic conditions.
The #1 preventable cause of denial
