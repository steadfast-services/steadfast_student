The 3 Systemic Blockers We Resolve
🔗
Weak Home-Country Ties
Under Section 214(b), every applicant is presumed to intend to immigrate. You must prove compelling reasons to return — most applicants fail to build this case with evidence.
Cited in 60%+ of 214(b) denials
💰
Financial Documentation Failures
Bank statements that look “too new,” funds with no clear source, or sponsor letters that don't match I-20 requirements can end an application instantly.
Flagged in majority of I-20 delays
🗣
Interview Unpreparedness
Consular officers make decisions in 2–3 minutes. Hesitation, inconsistency, or vague answers on career goals are red flags. Most applicants have never practiced under realistic conditions.
