## The Problem Nobody Is Talking About

Every year, thousands of qualified students from Nigeria, Pakistan, Ghana, India, Bangladesh,
and across the world apply for a U.S. student visa. They have the grades. They have the dream.
They do not get through.

Not because they were unqualified.
Because no one told them what actually matters in a consular interview.
Because no one explained that bank statement formatting can be the difference between approval and denial.
Because the U.S. visa system is not designed to be transparent — and most students are guessing.

**The numbers:**

| Country      | Approximate Annual Visa Refusal Rate |
|--------------|--------------------------------------|
| Nigeria      | ~63%                                 |
| Pakistan     | ~54%                                 |
| Ghana        | ~52%                                 |
| Bangladesh   | ~46%                                 |
| Vietnam      | ~38%                                 |
| Nepal        | ~36%                                 |
| India        | ~26%                                 |

*Source: U.S. Dept. of State, Bureau of Consular Affairs*

When a student is denied, they lose:

- **$350** SEVIS fee — non-refundable
- **$185** visa application fee — non-refundable
- **$500–$3,000** in school application deposits
- **12 months** waiting for the next intake cycle

A single denial costs the average student **$1,000–$3,500 and one full year of their life.**
