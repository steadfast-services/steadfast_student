Section 01
Executive Summary
A clear picture of who we are, what we do, and why we win.

Steadfast Student Services is a U.S.-based international education consulting company that guides students from 20+ countries through every step of the U.S. university enrollment journey — from initial academic screening and university matching to visa interview coaching and pre-departure orientation.

We operate a dual-channel revenue model: a Backend Commission Model for lower-risk student markets (Latin America, East Asia) and an Upfront Retainer Model for higher-risk markets (South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa). This structure ensures revenue stability regardless of visa volatility in any single geography.

Core Thesis: Low-risk markets generate predictable volume commissions. High-risk markets generate upfront cash flow. Together they fund a self-sustaining business that is insulated from single-country policy shifts.

Competitive Landscape

Large Agency Networks

High volume, low personalization. Students are processed at scale — often assigned to generalist counselors with no country-specific visa expertise. Our edge: Hyper-personalized coaching with visa-interview specialization.

Local In-Country Agents

Strong on ground relationships but weak on U.S.-side compliance and I-20/SEVIS expertise. Many operate without formal sub-agent credentials. Our edge: U.S.-based operations ensure full regulatory compliance and direct school relationships.

DIY Applicants

A growing segment using online resources — but they consistently underperform on visa interview outcomes due to lack of mock preparation and 214(b) objection coaching. Our edge: Our 3-round Visa Defense Program directly addresses this gap.

University-Direct Recruitment

Universities' own international offices are understaffed relative to applicant volume. They welcome compliant sub-agents who pre-screen qualified applicants. Our edge: We send schools pre-vetted, document-complete applicants — making us a valued pipeline.

Section 04
Service Framework
The end-to-end student lifecycle — from first contact to campus arrival.
1

Pre-Screening & Eligibility Evaluation

Conduct a structured intake interview and academic profile audit. Review GPA transcripts, English language proficiency scores (IELTS, TOEFL, Duolingo English Test), proof of financial capacity (liquid funds: minimum $20,000–$30,000 accessible), and personal academic goals. Use a proprietary scoring rubric to assign a student a Placement Viability Score (PVS) and determine which risk tier applies. Applicants who fail minimum academic thresholds are respectfully declined to protect agency reputation with university partners.

2

University Alignment & Match Strategy

Using the student's academic profile, financial capacity, and intended field of study, construct a balanced application portfolio: 2–3 reach institutions, 3–4 target institutions, and 2 safety institutions — all with active F-1 pipelines. Leverage aggregator portals (ApplyBoard, Adventus) for direct application submission. Prioritize schools with conditional admission pathways, on-campus housing for international students, and strong OPT/CPT records in the student's intended field.

3

Document Assembly & Application Management

Coach the student through drafting their Statement of Purpose (SOP) — we guide, they write. Format resumes per U.S. academic standards. Coordinate Letters of Recommendation timelines. Audit all official transcripts for format compliance. Submit formal applications through aggregator portals and track status in the Steadfast CRM. Provide one round of SOP structural feedback per application cycle. All editing respects academic integrity boundaries — no ghostwriting.

4

I-20 & SEVIS Processing Support

Once admitted, guide families through the financial verification documents required by the university's International Student Office (ISO) to issue Form I-20. This includes bank statements, affidavit of support, and sponsor letters formatted to the school's specific requirements. Ensure the student's SEVIS record is created and the SEVIS fee (I-901) is paid before the DS-160 visa application is filed. Liaise directly with the school's Designated School Official (DSO) where aggregator relationship permits.

5

The Visa Defense Program (3-Round Mock Protocol)

This is Steadfast's flagship differentiator. Run three structured mock consular interviews across three weeks, each targeting a progressively harder set of Section 214(b) objection scenarios:

  • Round 1 — Baseline: Core intent questions, home-country ties, financial sponsors
  • Round 2 — Stress Test: Career path logic, choice-of-school rationale, timeline credibility
  • Round 3 — Full Simulation: Adversarial consular role-play with recorded debrief and scoring

Each round is conducted via video call and recorded for review. Students receive a written action plan identifying their three highest-risk answer patterns and how to address each.

6

Pre-Departure Orientation

A 90-minute virtual session covering: airport arrival procedures, on-campus check-in expectations, Social Security Number application, banking setup, DSO first-week reporting requirement, and cultural transition coaching. Distribute a Steadfast Pre-Departure Handbook (branded PDF). This touchpoint also serves as an alumni activation moment — students are invited to join the Steadfast Ambassador Program upon graduation.

Add-On Services (Referral Revenue): Language training (IELTS/TOEFL prep partners), student housing (UniAcco, Amber), health insurance (ISO-compliant student plans), airport pickup coordination, and international SIM card partnerships.

Section 09
Compliance & Risk Management
Operating with full legal integrity protects the business, the students, and the university partnerships.
🧾

1099 & Tax Compliance

All income tracked through Schedule C (Form 1040). Issue Form W-9 to each aggregator partner. 1099-NEC to all sub-contractor referral agents earning over $600/year. Maintain quarterly estimated tax payments to avoid IRS penalties. Strongly recommended: retain a CPA with international business experience.

📋

F-1 OPT Work Authorization

If the principal is on F-1 OPT: business CIP code must match declared major. School's DSO must log minimum 20 hrs/week of business activity inside SEVP portal. Self-employment on OPT is permitted; ensure all income is properly attributed. Transition to O-1B (extraordinary ability) or EB-1A is a viable long-term immigration pathway as business scales.

✍️

Client Service Agreements

Every client signs a written Service Agreement before work begins. Agreement must state clearly: (1) visa decisions rest entirely with the U.S. Department of State; (2) Steadfast does not guarantee admissions or visa approvals; (3) refund policy is explicitly defined per package type; (4) academic integrity clause prohibiting ghostwriting of essays or fabrication of documents.

🔒

Data Privacy & AML Awareness

All student documents (transcripts, financial statements, passports) stored in encrypted Google Drive folders with access restricted to the assigned advisor. Never store original documents — certified copies only. Payments above $10,000 should be flagged in accordance with U.S. AML guidelines. International wire transfers documented with source-of-funds confirmation.

Ethical Boundaries (Non-Negotiable)

Steadfast will never: write student essays or personal statements directly · forge, alter, or "enhance" financial documents · guarantee visa issuance or university admission · coach students to misrepresent facts in visa interviews · accept payments through unverified or informal money transfer channels
